SMT-COMP 2011

The 2011 edition of the Satisfiability Modulo Theories Competition (SMT-COMP) is a satellite event of CAV 2011 (July_14_-_20, 2011, Snowbird, Utah).

News

  • Rules for this year's SMT-COMP posted
  • Application track benchmarks and tools posted for comment
  • SMT-LIBv2 parser available
  • SMT-LIBv2 tutorial and resources (thanks to GrammaTech's David Cok!)

Acknowledgments

SMT-COMP is supported by the U.S. National Science Foundation, under grant CNS-0551697. This grant has purchased the cluster on which the competition runs, as well as provided partial support for staff at the institution running the competition.

The in development (but not used for 2011) SMT-COMP benchmark execution infrastructure (STAR-Exec) is implemented by Aaron Stump and students. StarExec is hosted at The University of Iowa, thanks to Aaron Stump.
