SMT-COMP 2007

The Satisfiability Modulo Theories Competition (SMT-COMP) is a satellite event of CAV'07 (July 3 - 7, 2007, Berlin, Germany).

Organizers

  • Clark Barrett : Computer Science, New York University
  • Albert Oliveras : LSI Department, Technical University of Catalonia
  • Aaron Stump : Computer Science Engineering, Washington University in St. Louis

Acknowledgments

SMT-COMP is supported by the following funders:

  • Microsoft Research, providing a travel grant for competition participants, especially students, who might otherwise be unable to attend. See the travel grant page.
  • The U.S. National Science Foundation, under grant CNS-0551697. This grant has purchased the cluster on which the competition runs, as well as provided partial support for staff at the organizers' institutions running the competition.

The SMT-COMP benchmark execution infrastructure is implemented by:

  • Morgan Deters: Washington University in St. Louis.

An earlier version was written by Jon Fox (Washington University in St. Louis).
